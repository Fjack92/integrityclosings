Probates

Probate is a legal process in which a will is admitted to the court of law and accepted as a valid public document that is the true last testament of the deceased. If deceased did not leave a will, then the estate is settled according to the laws of intestacy. After the validity of the will is proven or intestacy is determined, the assets of the estate can be distributed to their heirs. Florida law has two main types of probates; Summary Administration and Formal Administration.

Summary Administration: This type of probate is used when the estate of the deceased does not exceed $75,000 (do not include homestead value). Our office can assist you with completing the required documents to successfully handle your own probate matter.
Fee: $750, plus filing and recording costs

Formal Administration: This type of probate is used when the estate value of the deceased exceeds $75,000.
Florida law only allows this type of administration pro-se (without attorney) if there is one heir to inherit the estate. If more than one heir inherits the estate, please contact a local attorney for assistance.
Fee: $1050, plus filing and recording costs

Deeds

A deed is a written document that is used to transfer real estate property. Our office cam assist with the preparation of the deed and electronic recording in the county where property is located.

Warranty Deed: A warranty deed provides the greatest amount of protection to the grantee of a property. It warrants that the owner owns the property free and clear of any outstanding liens, mortgages, or other encumbrances.
Fee: $125, plus costs

Quit Claim Deed: A quit claim deed makes no promises at all about the owner’s title. It transfers whatever interest the owner may have without any warranties. This does not guarantee that there are no other owners, or that there are no claims against the property.
Fee: $125, plus costs

Ladybird (Life Estate Deed): Ladybird deed gives the owner continued control over the property until his or her death. Once the owner dies, the property is transferred automatically to remaindermen without the need for probate.
Fee: $225, plus costs

Last Will & Testament

Last Will & Testament is the most common document used to specify how an estate should be settled after death. 

A will may name a guardian or trustee who will take care of a minor child if there is no surviving parent; be used to disinherit a child or to dictate who should receive specific items, such as artwork or jewelry. 

If one passes without a will the Florida intestate (without will) law determines how your assets will be divided.
Fee: $200

Power of Attorney is a legal document that authorizes one person to act on behalf of another person.  A person giving a Power of Attorney may make it very broad or may limit it to certain specific acts.

Durable allows the agent appointed to assist you with financial needs if you are unable to handle matters on your own, or if you become incompetent.

Health Surrogate appoints someone to make healthcare decisions for you when you become unable to make them for yourself.
